# Rewrite Patterns

## Pattern 1: Generic to concrete

Weak:

`A powerful platform for modern teams.`

Better:

`Automate repetitive ops work so your team ships faster with fewer manual handoffs.`

Why it works:

- Names the work
- Names the outcome
- Removes empty adjectives

## Pattern 2: Feature-first to outcome-first

Weak:

`Advanced workflow automation with customizable triggers.`

Better:

`Set up automations in minutes so follow-ups, handoffs, and alerts happen automatically.`

Why it works:

- Leads with what changes for the buyer
- Keeps the mechanism secondary

## Pattern 3: Broad claim to believable claim

Weak:

`Increase productivity across your organization.`

Better:

`Cut recurring admin work so reps spend more time selling instead of updating tools.`

Why it works:

- Narrows the audience and scenario
- Gives the claim a believable mechanism

## Pattern 4: Weak CTA to value CTA

Weak:

`Submit`

Better:

`See how much time you can save`

Why it works:

- Reduces ambiguity
- Reinforces the benefit at the point of action

## Pattern 5: Category label to problem statement

Weak:

`Lead management software`

Better:

`Stop losing leads because follow-up slipped through the cracks.`

Why it works:

- Starts with pain
- Gives the reader a reason to care immediately

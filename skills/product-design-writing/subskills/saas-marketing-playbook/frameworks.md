# Frameworks

## Positioning Core

Use this sequence:

1. Customer
2. Pain
3. Desired outcome
4. Alternative
5. Differentiation
6. Proof

Good positioning should make it easy to answer:

- Who is this for?
- What problem does it solve?
- What happens if the buyer does nothing?
- Why this option over another?
- Why should the buyer believe the claim?

## Messaging Hierarchy

Order messaging from most important to least important:

1. Primary promise
2. Why it matters
3. How it works at a high level
4. Proof
5. Objection handling
6. Call to action

If a page leads with product taxonomy or feature buckets before the buyer understands the outcome, the hierarchy is usually wrong.

## Conversion Copy Formula

Use this structure for high-intent sections:

1. Problem
2. Cost of problem
3. Desired outcome
4. Solution
5. Proof
6. CTA

## Rewrite Heuristics

- Outcome before mechanism
- Specific before abstract
- Proof before adjectives
- Customer language before internal jargon
- One strong claim before many weak claims

## Differentiation Checks

Ask:

- Could three competitors say the same sentence?
- Does the claim depend on generic words rather than real differences?
- Is the contrast against the buyer's current alternative explicit?

If the answer is yes, the messaging is probably not differentiated enough.
